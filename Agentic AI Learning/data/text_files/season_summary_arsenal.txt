
The 2025/26 season was arguably the breakthrough year of the Arsenal F.C. project under Mikel Arteta. After several seasons of finishing as runners-up, Arsenal finally won the Premier League, ending a 22-year wait for a league title.

Premier League: Mission Accomplished

Arsenal finished 1st in the league with a record of 26 wins, 7 draws, and 5 defeats, finishing ahead of Manchester City F.C.. The title was secured after years of near-misses and established Arteta's side as the dominant domestic team of the season.

Some key themes of the league campaign:

Greater maturity in big matches.
Improved squad depth compared to previous seasons.
Defensive consistency led by players like William Saliba and Gabriel Magalhães.
Huge influence from Declan Rice and Martin Ødegaard.
Strong performances from new arrivals and attacking reinforcements.
Champions League: So Close to European Glory

Arsenal also reached the UEFA Champions League final against Paris Saint-Germain F.C.. They led early through Kai Havertz but PSG equalized and eventually won on penalties. It was a painful ending, but reaching the final represented Arsenal's strongest European run in many years.

Transfer Impact

The club significantly strengthened the squad during the season:

Viktor Gyökeres arrived and became one of the headline signings.
Eberechi Eze returned to the club.
Martin Zubimendi added control in midfield.
Additional depth signings helped Arsenal compete across multiple competitions.
Statistical Highlights

Fans celebrated several remarkable achievements:

First Premier League title since 2003/04.
No penalties conceded and no red cards received in the league campaign.
Strong performances in both domestic and European competitions.
Arsenal spent more days at the top of the table than any other side over the last several seasons.
Fan Sentiment

The mood around the club was a mix of relief, pride, and belief that this team has finally crossed the psychological barrier that had stopped previous title challenges. Many supporters viewed the title as validation of Arteta's long-term rebuild after enduring criticism during earlier seasons.

Season Grade: A

Even with the Champions League final defeat, winning the Premier League after a 22-year drought makes 2025/26 one of the most successful Arsenal seasons of the modern era. The next challenge is turning one title into a sustained period of dominance while continuing to compete for European honours.
